  • My School Project Overview

    Since I can remember, the thought of a magic mirror has always captivated my imagination.

    Combining ancient enchantment with modern technology, it's a incredible thing. Now, i get to try it with a Raspberry Pi, I am embarking on a journey to create my very own magic mirror. This project serves as a cherished memory and a blueprint for future endeavors, allowing me to recreate this magical experience time and again.

  • Work Development Each week

    So the first week went to planing the project and deciding what project I wanted to do. During this week I also decided that I wanted to do a "magic mirror", the next week we had to have a list with the parts needed so I started on that.

    During the second week I had my project accepted and had the parts bought and received, I had to purchase a two way mirror. I had trouble getting a two way mirror so I instead bought a picture frame and some "two way" film, the film is originally made to be as a way to have privacy in your home.

    The last week was hard, one of the cables that I needed ended up breaking. I also could not get the face id to work and in the end I decided to focus on my website instead, this project has been fun even with all the problems i've had but I obviously whished to have finished it.

  • Problems that arose

    During the first week my main problem was downloading everything that I needed, I kept getting error messages but after changing wifi the errors were gone.

    My second problem arrived shortly after however, while trying to download Node.js my raspberry kept freezing and the day was finished. I continued to download it the next thursday.

    Soon another problem came too, the hdmi cable broke...I need another cable but I don't know when i'll get it. I also did not get the face recognition to work, so i've decided to do the rest of the modules and if I have time try again.

  • Project Finish

    Sadly I did not get to finish this project, I had to many thing go wrong and too little time.
    One of my theories about why I did not get to finish this project, is the fact that I used an touchscreen instead of an IR frame.
